About

My research focuses on applying large language models (LLMs) to software engineering, combining advanced techniques such as RAG, LLM-based agents, prompt engineering, tool-augmented reasoning, PEFT, and dataset construction and evaluation. I combine these approaches with empirical studies on real-world datasets from GitHub and Stack Overflow, exploring how AI can effectively support developers, improve software architecture decisions, and enhance productivity.

I am particularly interested in:
• AI-assisted software development: helping developers work more efficiently and effectively.
• AI-assisted software architecture design: supporting architectural decision-making and recovering missing or implicit design rationale.
• AI-assisted program repair: detecting, localizing, and repairing software defects.

Beyond research, I enjoy hands-on engineering and have experience building data pipelines, developing prototypes with Python and Go, and deploying full-stack applications, bridging AI research with practical software engineering.
